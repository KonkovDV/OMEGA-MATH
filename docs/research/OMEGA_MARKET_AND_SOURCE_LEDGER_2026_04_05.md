# OMEGA Market And Source Ledger

## Purpose

This file exists to keep OMEGA startup materials source-backed.

Do not place large market numbers or strategic claims into pitch-style docs unless they are first logged here with:
- source
- date
- evidence type
- what the source can safely support
- what it cannot safely support

## Usage Rules

1. Official guidance beats recollection.
2. Vendor reports are acceptable for pain framing and market signals, but not as neutral proof of market share or universal performance claims.
3. If a source supports only qualitative positioning, do not convert it into a hard revenue number.
4. If a source is good enough only for C1, say so.

## Working Interpretation For OMEGA

### What we can say today

1. Seed investors expect clarity, focus, and believable milestones.
2. Risk-scoped assurance language is stronger than absolute safety language.
3. Formal verification is already a real commercial workflow category.
4. Web3 security offers a plausible initial pain wedge.
5. OMEGA's current repo reality better supports a bounded workflow thesis than a universal AI oracle thesis.

### What we cannot honestly say yet

1. OMEGA has a verified TAM model with defensible revenue numbers.
2. OMEGA is already enterprise-ready for regulated sectors.
3. OMEGA has proven competitive superiority over specialist vendors.
4. OMEGA has customer-validated ROI.

## Source-Backed Statements Allowed In Near-Term Startup Docs

Allowed examples:
- `Current formal verification markets demonstrate that buyers already pay for specification-first assurance workflows.`
- `Public Web3 exploit pressure makes security review economically legible.`
- `AI governance guidance increasingly favors traceability, documentation, oversight, and scoped claims.`

Disallowed examples until more evidence exists:
- `OMEGA is the first system to solve hallucinations in critical software.`
- `OMEGA guarantees correctness of customer logic.`
- `OMEGA is ready for aerospace, medtech, and banking deployment.`
